## AI/AGI in Finance

The finance project focused on how Artificial General Intelligence could reshape banking, investing, lending, compliance, and customer protection. It explained that current AI in finance is mostly narrow AI, meaning it performs specific tasks such as fraud detection, credit scoring, customer support, risk management, and anti-money laundering monitoring. AGI would go beyond narrow AI by reasoning across multiple domains and adapting to new financial problems.

The proposed **Financial Guardian AGI Platform** would support personalized financial guidance, scam prevention, fairer lending, regulatory monitoring, and market risk analysis. The main benefits would include improved efficiency, stronger fraud protection, better financial access, and clearer decision support. The main concerns would include data privacy, cybersecurity, algorithmic bias, overdependence on automation, and impacts on financial jobs.

## AI in Government and Public Sector

The government project focused on the U.S. Treasury's use of machine learning to prevent and recover fraud and improper payments. The case showed that AI can help government agencies analyze large amounts of payment data, detect suspicious activity, support investigators, and protect taxpayer money. The key advantage of AI in this setting is that it can identify patterns faster than manual review alone.

The proposed **AI Civic Signal Platform** would help local governments detect service problems early by analyzing citizen service requests, public feedback, city maintenance records, weather alerts, and geospatial patterns. The expected benefits include faster service response, better resource allocation, improved public trust, and stronger support for underserved communities. Risks include privacy concerns, biased data, false alerts, and the need for human oversight.

## Combined Insight

Both projects show that AI is most valuable when it supports human decision-making instead of replacing it. In finance, AI can protect people from fraud and improve access to financial guidance. In government, AI can protect public funds and help agencies respond faster to community needs. The biggest requirement in both areas is responsible design, including privacy protection, fairness, transparency, explainability, cybersecurity, and human oversight.
